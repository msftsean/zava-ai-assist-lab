# Radio Communications and Check-In Procedures

**Document ID:** SOP-RC-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2024-03-15
**Review Date:** 2025-03-15
**Owner:** Communications Division Lead

---

## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the policies and procedures for conducting radio communications and scheduled check-in procedures across all operational units. It ensures consistent, reliable, and secure radio communication practices during routine operations, field deployments, and emergency situations.

## 2. Scope

Applies to:

- All field personnel and dispatchers
- All operational shifts and duty rotations
- Portable, mobile, and base-station radio equipment
- Both encrypted and unencrypted communication channels

## 3. Radio Communication Fundamentals

### 3.1 Authorised Frequencies and Channels

| Channel | Frequency | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Channel 1 (Primary)** | 155.475 MHz | Day-to-day operations and routine traffic |
| **Channel 2 (Tactical)** | 155.745 MHz | Field operations and tactical coordination |
| **Channel 3 (Emergency)** | 155.160 MHz | Emergency traffic only |
| **Channel 4 (Admin)** | 156.000 MHz | Administrative and logistics coordination |
| **Channel 5 (Interop)** | 154.935 MHz | Inter-agency interoperability |

### 3.2 Call Signs

- All personnel must use assigned call signs during radio transmissions.
- Call signs follow the format: **[Unit Prefix]-[Number]** (e.g., ALPHA-1, BRAVO-7).
- Dispatch is identified as **"Base"** or **"Dispatch"**.
- Supervisors use their unit prefix followed by **"-Lead"** (e.g., ALPHA-Lead).

### 3.3 Transmission Etiquette

1. **Listen before transmitting** — ensure the channel is clear.
2. **Press and hold** the Push-To-Talk (PTT) button for one full second before speaking.
3. **Speak clearly** at a moderate pace; avoid jargon not on the approved code list.
4. **Keep transmissions brief** — limit messages to 30 seconds or less.
5. **Use the phonetic alphabet** (NATO) for spelling out words, names, or codes.
6. **End each transmission** with your call sign (e.g., "ALPHA-1, out").

## 4. Scheduled Check-In Procedures

### 4.1 Check-In Types

| Check-In Type | Frequency | Required By |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Shift Start Check-In** | Once per shift | All personnel at shift start |
| **Hourly Status Check** | Every 60 minutes | All field personnel |
| **Welfare Check** | Every 30 minutes | Lone workers and isolated posts |
| **End-of-Shift Check-Out** | Once per shift | All personnel at shift end |

### 4.2 Shift Start Check-In Procedure

1. At the beginning of each shift, all personnel must radio in to Dispatch (Base).
2. Use the following format:
   - **"Base, this is [Call Sign], checking in for [shift designation], [number of personnel], [current location], [equipment status]. Over."**
   - Example: *"Base, this is ALPHA-1, checking in for Day Shift, two personnel, posted at Sector 4, all equipment operational. Over."*
3. Dispatch will acknowledge with:
   - **"[Call Sign], Base copies. You are logged on at [time]. Base out."**
4. If Dispatch does not acknowledge within **60 seconds**, repeat the check-in. After **three failed attempts**, switch to Channel 3 (Emergency) and attempt contact.

### 4.3 Hourly Status Check Procedure

1. Dispatch will initiate hourly roll-call on Channel 1 at the top of each hour.
2. Dispatch announces: **"All units, this is Base. Hourly status check. Respond in sequence."**
3. Each unit responds in assigned order:
   - **"Base, [Call Sign], [status code]. Over."**
4. Approved status codes:

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **10-4** | Operational, no issues |
| **10-6** | Busy, stand by |
| **10-7** | Out of service (specify reason) |
| **10-20** | Reporting current location |
| **10-33** | Emergency traffic — clear channel |

5. If a unit fails to respond, Dispatch must:
   - Attempt **two additional calls** at 2-minute intervals.
   - If still unresponsive, initiate the **Welfare Check Escalation Protocol** (see Section 5).

### 4.4 Welfare Check Procedure (Lone Workers)

1. Lone workers and isolated-post personnel must check in every **30 minutes**.
2. Use the format: **"Base, [Call Sign], welfare check, [status code], [location]. Over."**
3. Dispatch logs each welfare check with timestamp and status.
4. If a lone worker misses a scheduled welfare check:
   - Dispatch attempts contact **three times** over 5 minutes.
   - If no response, Dispatch escalates to the Shift Supervisor immediately.
   - The Shift Supervisor dispatches a **buddy team** to the last known location within **15 minutes**.

### 4.5 End-of-Shift Check-Out Procedure

1. At the end of each shift, all personnel must radio Dispatch:
   - **"Base, this is [Call Sign], checking out from [shift designation], [any incidents to report]. Over."**
   - Example: *"Base, this is BRAVO-3, checking out from Night Shift, no incidents to report. Over."*
2. Dispatch acknowledges and logs the check-out time.
3. Personnel must **not** leave their post until Dispatch confirms the check-out.

## 5. Escalation Protocols

### 5.1 Missed Check-In Escalation

| Elapsed Time | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| **0–5 minutes** | Dispatch makes three contact attempts on primary channel |
| **5–10 minutes** | Dispatch attempts contact on all available channels |
| **10–15 minutes** | Shift Supervisor notified; buddy team dispatched |
| **15–30 minutes** | Operations Manager notified; GPS tracking activated |
| **30+ minutes** | Incident Commander activated; emergency response initiated |

### 5.2 Emergency Radio Procedures

1. If an emergency arises during any radio communication:
   - Transmit **"MAYDAY, MAYDAY, MAYDAY"** on Channel 3 (Emergency).
   - State: **"This is [Call Sign], [nature of emergency], [location], [personnel count], [assistance required]. Over."**
2. All non-emergency traffic on Channel 3 must **cease immediately** upon hearing a MAYDAY call.
3. Dispatch assumes coordination and logs the emergency start time.
4. Emergency communications take absolute priority over all other radio traffic.

## 6. Equipment Requirements

### 6.1 Radio Equipment Checks

Before each shift, personnel must verify:

- Battery is fully charged (minimum 80% at shift start).
- Antenna is securely attached and undamaged.
- All programmed channels are accessible.
- Volume is set appropriately for the environment.
- Encryption is enabled on channels that require it.

### 6.2 Backup Communication

- Each unit must carry a **backup battery** or portable charger.
- Mobile phone numbers for all team members must be pre-loaded as a secondary communication method.
- In the event of total radio failure, personnel must proceed to the nearest **rally point** and report in person.

## 7. Documentation and Record-Keeping

- Dispatch must maintain a **radio communications log** for each shift.
- The log must record: timestamp, call sign, check-in type, status code, and any notable remarks.
- Logs are retained for a minimum of **12 months** in the shift management system.
- Missed check-in incidents must be documented as **Incident Reports** within 24 hours.

## 8. Training Requirements

- All personnel must complete **Radio Communications Certification** before being assigned a call sign.
- Annual refresher training is mandatory and covers:
  - Proper radio etiquette and transmission procedures
  - Check-in protocols and escalation procedures
  - Emergency communication procedures
  - Equipment care and troubleshooting
- Training records are maintained by the Communications Division.

## 9. Compliance and Enforcement

- Failure to conduct scheduled check-ins is a **safety violation** subject to progressive discipline.
- Unauthorised use of emergency channels is prohibited and subject to disciplinary action.
- Radio communication logs are subject to audit by the Safety and Compliance team quarterly.
